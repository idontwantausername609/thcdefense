Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common concerns about criminal defense and marijuana-related charges in Texas.

Do I need a lawyer if I haven’t been charged yet?

Yes. Early intervention can protect your rights and sometimes prevent charges from being filed altogether.

Will a marijuana arrest appear on my record?

Yes, unless it is sealed or expunged. Even dismissed cases will show up unless you take action to remove them.

Can I be arrested for medical marijuana?

Possibly. Texas has strict limitations, and non-approved medical use may still lead to arrest.

How long does a nondisclosure or expunction take?

Most cases are resolved in 1 to 6 months, depending on the court and required documentation.

What happens at a first court appearance?

You will be informed of your charges, advised of rights, and asked to enter a plea. A lawyer can appear with or for you.

What should I do if I’m stopped by police and questioned about marijuana?

Stay calm, be polite, and exercise your right to remain silent. Do not consent to searches. Ask if you are free to leave.

Can a marijuana conviction affect my employment or education?

Yes. Even minor convictions can impact job opportunities, professional licenses, and eligibility for student aid.

Is CBD legal in Texas?

CBD products with less than 0.3% THC are legal, but products with higher THC content are not.

What’s the difference between possession and intent to distribute?

Possession means you have marijuana for personal use. Intent to distribute involves evidence you planned to sell or share it, which carries harsher penalties.

Can I get my marijuana case dismissed?

It’s possible, depending on the facts of your case, police conduct, and available defenses. We can help you explore your options.

Will I lose my driver’s license if convicted?

A conviction for certain drug offenses can result in a driver’s license suspension.

How much marijuana is considered a felony in Texas?

Possession of more than four ounces is typically charged as a felony.

What are my rights during a search of my car or home?

You have the right to refuse consent to a search. Police generally need a warrant or probable cause.

Can juveniles be charged for marijuana offenses?

Yes. Juveniles face different procedures, but charges can still have serious consequences.

How can I clear my record after a marijuana arrest?

You may be eligible for expunction or nondisclosure. We can advise you on the process.
