Marijuana Laws

A clear explanation of the current cannabis laws in Texas and federal law.

Is Marijuana Legal in Texas?

No. Possession of marijuana remains illegal in Texas for recreational use. However, certain medical uses under the Compassionate Use Program are allowed in very limited circumstances.

What Are the Penalties?

Texas classifies marijuana possession by weight:

  • Under 2 oz: Class B misdemeanor (up to 180 days in jail and $2,000 fine)
  • 2–4 oz: Class A misdemeanor
  • Over 4 oz: Felony charges, increasing by weight

What About CBD and Delta-8?

CBD is legal if it contains less than 0.3% THC. Delta-8 exists in a gray area — some courts and prosecutors treat it as illegal, others don’t. Legal challenges are ongoing.

Can I Be Charged for THC Vape Pens or Edibles?

Yes. Under Texas law, THC oil and edibles are considered controlled substances (penalty group 2), often resulting in felony charges — even for small amounts.

What Does Federal Law Say?

Marijuana is still illegal under federal law. Federal charges are rare for individuals, but possession on federal land or airports can trigger enforcement.

Is Legalization Coming?

While national trends lean toward legalization, Texas has not passed meaningful recreational cannabis reform. Legislative efforts have failed repeatedly in recent sessions.

What Should I Do If I’m Arrested?

Do not admit guilt or discuss your case with police. Contact a defense attorney immediately. Your rights and record may still be protected.
